Hardware care

For locks that need fitting, repair, or replacement.

Installation and repair requests often involve hardware that no longer works smoothly: a deadbolt drags, a latch misses the strike, a key sticks, a handle feels loose, or an older lock needs replacement. The correct route depends on whether the issue is caused by the lock, the door, the frame, or prior damage.

This service type helps organize lock replacement, deadbolt installation, cylinder repair, strike plate adjustment, and general door hardware correction.

When needed

New hardware, worn locks, broken cylinders, sticky keys, loose handles, or doors that do not close securely.

What is reviewed

Door prep, backset, bore size, strike alignment, latch throw, lock grade, and hardware compatibility.

Expected outcome

A clearer decision between adjustment, repair, replacement, reinforcement, or new lock installation.

Trust context

Repair-first thinking can reduce unnecessary replacement when the lock or door can be corrected.

Process

Hardware issues need the right diagnosis.

Not every lock problem means the lock itself is bad.

01

Describe the symptom

Sticky key, loose handle, deadbolt drag, spinning cylinder, or misaligned latch all mean different things.

02

Identify the door type

Wood, metal, glass storefront, exterior, interior, or gate hardware may affect compatibility.

03

Note existing hardware

Brand, smart lock model, deadbolt type, and visible damage help frame the request.

04

Choose repair or install

The request can point toward adjustment, part repair, full replacement, or new installation.

Request details

Hardware work depends on fit, wear, and door movement.

Many lock problems are caused by the door or frame rather than the lock alone. A deadbolt that scrapes the strike, a latch that misses, or a handle that loosens repeatedly may point to alignment, hardware age, or installation fit.

A useful request explains the symptom, the door type, whether the lock ever worked properly, and whether the goal is repair, replacement, reinforcement, or a new installation.

Common hardware scenarios

  • Deadbolts that stick, drag, or fail to extend fully
  • Loose knobs, handlesets, cylinders, or strike plates
  • New lock installation after renovation or damage
  • Replacement of outdated or unreliable entry hardware

Helpful details to include

  • Door material and whether it is exterior or interior
  • Lock brand or visible model if available
  • Whether the key turns, binds, spins, or stops halfway
  • Photos of the lock, strike plate, and door edge

What can affect scope

  • Warped doors, sagging hinges, or frame movement
  • Old bore holes or non-standard door preparation
  • Compatibility between new hardware and existing cutouts
  • Need for reinforcement after forced entry or wear
FAQ

Install and repair questions.

Common hardware concerns.

Why does my deadbolt stick?

Common causes include door movement, strike misalignment, worn hardware, paint buildup, or cylinder issues.

Can a damaged lock be repaired?

Sometimes. It depends on the damage, lock type, part availability, and security expectations.

Should I upgrade to a higher-grade lock?

That depends on door use, property type, exposure, and the level of durability or key control desired.

Need lock installation or repair guidance?

Share the hardware issue and door type so the request can be sorted clearly.
